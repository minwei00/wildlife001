Night Safari

Unravel the mysterious world of Night Safari — the world’s first nocturnal wildlife park and 13-time Best Attraction Experience winner in Singapore.

As night falls, explore 4 walking trails and ride our Safari Adventure Tram to meet over 900 nocturnal creatures, from free-roaming Malayan tapirs to our majestic lions. Dare to explore the wild after dark?

Opening Hours:
Daily, 6:00pm – 12:00am (Last Entry: 11:15pm)

River Wonders

Join us for a voyage beyond the cascading waters, and into the mesmerising world of River Wonders — Asia's first and only river-themed wildlife park.

Home to one of the world’s largest collections of freshwater vertebrates, be enchanted by the some of the most peculiar and fascinating river-dwelling animals, like our resident giants — the Indian gharial, Mekong giant catfish and giant pandas!

Opening Hours:
Daily, 9:00am – 6:00pm (Last Entry: 5:00pm)

Bird Paradise

Immerse in a symphony of colours at Bird Paradise — Asia’s largest bird park.

Spanning 17 hectares, Bird Paradise is home to more than 3,500 birds from over 400 avian species with 8 large thematic walk-through aviaries. Each aviary reflects unique features, vegetation and bird species from different biomes around the world. Flock down to Bird Paradise today!

Opening Hours:
Daily, 9:00am – 6:00pm (Last Entry: 5:00pm)

Mandai Boardwalk
Entrances Digital Trail Highlights Native Wildlife Wildlife Advisory Visitor Guide

Stretching 3.3km along the edges of River Wonders and Singapore Zoo, the Mandai Boardwalk offers stunning views across Upper Seletar Reservoir towards the Central Catchment Nature Reserve.

Designed for all ages and abilities, the boardwalk is a haven for native wildlife, where you may chance upon birds, reptiles, butterflies, and more.

Mandai Boardwalk is a freely accessible public space.

Opening Hours:
Daily, 7:00am – 7:00pm (Last entry at 6:00pm)

Highlights
Rest Stations

Need a break? Have a rest at one of the Boardwalk’s unique rest stations, each inspired by local wildlife.

Enjoy sweeping reservoir views at “Eagle’s Perch”, spot dragonflies at “Damselfly’s Wing”, or unwind at “Lizard’s Lounge”, inspired by the Malayan water monitor.

Native Wildlife Haven

Specifically designed to be a haven for native wildlife, the Mandai Boardwalk offers close chance encounters with the changeable hawk-eagle, crested goshawk, oriental whip snake, Sunda colugo, white-bellied sea eagle, Malayan water monitor, and more.

It’s also a perfect spot for birdwatching and photography—so keep your eyes peeled!

Iora’s Nest

“Iora’s Nest” is a serene lookout along the Mandai Boardwalk, inspired by the nests of the common iora.

Overlooking the reservoir and thriving grasslands, it’s the perfect spot to birdwatch, unwind, and soak in nature.

Long-tailed macaque

This omnivorous, seed-dispersing monkey frequents its surroundings to explore and forage for food. If you encounter them, please do not feed them!

Smooth-coated otter

Recognised by its shorter fur, more rounded head, and furless, diamond-shaped nose. If you spot otters, remember to maintain a respectful distance!

Changeable hawk-eagle

This agile forest-dwelling bird of prey gets its name from its variable appearance - pale to dark, due to age and molting.

Crested goshawk

Females are larger than males, with browner heads and under-streaks. Their flight has a characteristic "slow flap, slow flap, straight glide" pattern.

Green-crested lizard

While its body is usually bright green with a bluish tinge, its colour may change to a dark brown or grey when threatened.

Malayan water monitor

Considered to be the second-largest lizard species (after the Komodo dragon), it has up to two replacement teeth lying behind each tooth at a given time!

Oriental whip snake

Also known as the Asian vine snake. Although mildly venomous, this diurnal snake is not considered a threat to humans.

Sunda colugo

This arboreal and skilful climber glides among trees using its patagium – its kite-shaped skin that expands and connects from its neck to its limbs.

White-bellied sea eagle

While hunting over water on sunny days, this skilful bird has been known to fly at certain angles against the sun to avoid casting shadows and alerting prey!

White-throated kingfisher

Growing up to 28cm in length, these large kingfishers build their nests in riverbanks by making digging through mud walls with their bills.

Mandai Gallery

Step into Mandai Gallery, a permanent exhibition that celebrates the history and transformation of the Mandai Wildlife Reserve. From its humble beginnings as a zoo to becoming a world-leading wildlife and nature destination, the exhibition highlights our contribution to nation-building, our commitment to sustainability and our efforts in wildlife conservation both locally and regionally.

Through engaging multimedia displays, hear from our keepers and wildlife experts about how we care for the animals in our parks, and the conservation initiatives that support a thriving natural world. Mandai Gallery invites visitors to reflect on this journey and be inspired to join us in protecting wildlife for a sustainable future.

Located in the same building as Curiosity Cove, Mandai Gallery is open daily from 10:00am to 7:00pm.

Opening Hours: Daily, 10:00am – 7:00pm

Getting Here & Around

Highlights
Mandai Milestones: Then to Now
Discover Mandai’s journey from a modest zoo in the 1970s to a world-leading wildlife and nature destination. Through nostalgic photographs, personal anecdotes, and little-known facts, trace the milestones that have shaped our growth and ongoing vision for the future.
Wild To Wall
Be immersed in a powerful floor-to-ceiling multimedia show that brings our conservation journey to life. Discover how we care for animals in our parks, protect wildlife in Singapore and the region — all part of our commitment to preserving a thriving natural world for future generations.
Wild Encounters, Lasting Memories
Be captivated by personal stories of inidviduals and the iconic animal ambassadors that form the heart of Mandai. From unforgettable encounters with Inuka — the first polar bear born in the tropics, to the enduring legacy of Ah Meng, the orangutan — the beloved orangutan, hear how each generation has been touched by these remarkable moments at the Mandai Wildlife Reserve. See how these experiences inspire both visitors and conservationists to become stewards of wildlife.
Be a Voice for Wildlife
Pick your favourite animal ambassador, customise it with icons, and share your message for wildlife on a digital interactive wall to seal your commitment! Scan a QR code to express what conservation means to you, and join us in protecting the planet’s precious biodiversity.

Spot The Sculpture, Save The Species

This sculpture of a Sunda pangolin and its pangopup greets you at Mandai Wildlife EAST, offering a gentle reminder of the challenges these elusive creatures face.

Native to Singapore, Sunda pangolins often venture beyond forests, putting them at risk of traffic accidents. If you're driving near forested areas, remember to slow down—and if you ever spot an injured pangolin, contact ACRES for help.

A Safe Refuge
You can also learn more about Sunda pangolins and other creatures at Night Safari’s Pangolin Trail.

Past Events
Wild Day Out

In celebration of Singapore’s 60th birthday, families and friends came together for a joyful weekend on 28 and 29 June 2025 at Mandai Wildlife WEST for the second edition of Wild Day Out.

Held in partnership with The Artground, this special SG60 edition offered a vibrant line-up of activities — from heritage-inspired crafts like Five Stones and natural-dye postcards, to imaginative play performances thoughtfully curated for young children.

Guests also enjoyed complimentary tastings from our F&B tenants, while our furry friends joined in the fun with enriching pet-friendly workshops.

Find Out More
National Walk-Your-Dog Day

On 22 February 2025 (Saturday), Mandai Wildlife WEST together with Pawpaw by Mandai X hosted over 60 dogs and their owners for National Walk-Your-Dog-Day as part of Dogothon SG 2025.

Organised by WanderPaws SG, Dogathon SG 2025 is a fundraising event designed to bring the dog community together and encourage pawrents to bond with their dogs through an active, healthy lifestyle.

For 2 weeks, dog owners were invited to track their daily walks with their pets, culminating in a finale on 22 February 2025 — National Walk-Your-Dog Day — at the scenic Mandai Wildlife WEST.

Participants were gathered for the prize giving and sharings of how Pawpaw's expert-led guidance on both nutrition and training by Mandai stands to benefit pawrents.

The event ended with a pack walk around the scenic Mandai Wildlife WEST exploring the pet friendly precinct and ended with delicious pet friendly ice cream at the Mandai Wildlife Shop, catered for all things pets.

This meaningful event celebrated the joy of walking together while raising funds for Mercylight and Every Stray Matters (ESM).

Parks Rules and Conditions

The following rules and conditions apply to all visitors to the Mandai Wildlife Reserve (the "MWR"), which includes areas that require an Admission Ticket or Attraction Ticket (both as defined in the Park Tickets Terms and Conditions and collectively, the "Ticketed Areas") for their own safety and enjoyment, and that of others.

	
Respect Wildlife

You may encounter wildlife during your time with us, as the Mandai Wildlife Reserve is located within the Central Catchment Nature Reserve in Singapore.

Such wildlife is not within the care or control of Mandai Wildlife Group (as defined below). Do not attempt to approach, feed, touch, tease or do any act to cause disturbance, annoyance, or injury to both wildlife and our animals. 

Maintain a safe distance and appreciate them from afar.

	
Respect our Animals
	
Smoke-free Mandai Wildlife Reserve
	
No Flash Photography or Flashlights
	
Use of Active Mobility Devices (AMDs) in the Mandai Wildlife Reserve
	
Keep the Mandai Wildlife Reserve Clean
	
Pets and Service Animals in the Mandai Wildlife Reserve (but not the Ticketed Areas and Mandai Boardwalk)
	
Respect Plant Life
	
No Releasing of Animals
	
No Kite-flying and Playing of Frisbees or Balls
	
No Remote-Controlled Aerial Devices
	
No Open Fires
	
No Fishing or Swimming
	
No balloons in the Ticketed Areas and the Mandai Boardwalk
	
Strollers/Prams and PMAs
	
Explore Safely
	
Filming and Photography

Day Off in Singapore

싱가포르 DAY OFF! or DAY OFF IN SINGAPORE is a content series produced by WILD, partnered with the Mandai Wildlife Reserve and the Singapore Tourism Board.

The series features prominent faces including Sandara Park from 2NE1, The Hammington Family, and Park Minkyu from Netflix's Singles Inferno S3.

The series follows Korean celebrities as they take a day off from their busy schedules to explore the Mandai Wildlife Reserve, discovering Singapore through immersive wildlife experiences and nature.

Each episode introduces light-hearted missions, unexpected choices, and interactive encounters across our parks and experiences.

Ep 1: A Wild Day with Your Bestie

Featuring Sandara Park from 2NE1

Follow Sandara Park’s perfect bestie day out at the Singapore Zoo with Sorn, taking on surprise quests.

From Breakfast in the Wild and giraffe feeding to Fragile Forest and giant pandas at River Wonders, it’s a playful mix of fun, discovery, and laid-back moments.

Catch The Episode

Featuring The Hammington Family

Catch The Episode

Featuring Park Minkyu from Netflix’s Singles Inferno S3

Amazon River Quest
Spot These Animals Rider's Guidelines

Embark on an unforgettable boat adventure into a mysterious river wilderness at River Wonders. Designed to simulate a journey along the Amazon River, be ready to spot our well-camouflaged and elusive wildlife among lush vegetation for a unique perspective of Amazonian wildlife.

Amazon River Quest tickets are available for purchase online and onsite at $5 per rider. Last ride is at 5:30pm. For safety reasons, riders must be at least 1.06m to ride. See below for more rider's guidelines.

Spot These Animals

Be enthralled by the sights and sounds of animals hailing from one of the most diverse rainforest habitats in the world!

Giant Anteater

With its distinct narrow head, bushy tail and long snout, it's almost impossible to miss the giant anteater. As it shuffles around searching for food, the giant anteater uses its long and narrow tongue to lap up ants and termites from ant mounds.

Brazilian Tapir

Almost always found near water, this species of tapir is fast on land — and even faster in the water. Its multipurpose snout doubles up as a snorkel, helping the animal to stay submerged for hours.

Jaguar

In Amazon River Quest, be transported into a dark cave where you’ll get to encounter a spotted predator in the form of the jaguar. This stealthy cat is an excellent swimmer and is perfectly capable of carrying large prey even while swimming.

Height restrictions

Children who are 1.06m and taller are welcome to embark on Amazon River Quest. A child swap procedure is in place such that a supervising adult and the child can wait while the others go on the boat ride. The supervising adult can board the boat once the others return.

Amazon River Quest guidelines

The guidelines provided here are to ensure that you are able to make a well-informed decision about your ability to experience the boat ride safely. The following restrictions are put in place to protect the safety of our riders and to ensure that the safety systems of the ride are not compromised.

Riders must be 1.06m to take the boat ride
Children between 1.06m and 1.35m to be accompanied by a supervising adult
Riders must be able to transfer to boat unaided

Please also note that the boat ride involves moderate acceleration, elevation changes and minor bumping. As a result, guests with the following conditions shall not ride.

Expectant mothers
Motion sickness or dizziness
Heart conditions or abnormal blood pressure
Back or neck disorder
Guests with impaired physical health

To ride safely, riders must follow these boat rules:

No food and drinks
No smoking
No loose items
No bulky items
No sharp objects
No sitting on the lap
Keep hands and legs within the boat
Remain seated at all times

Macaque alert

Wild macaques in our attractions associate baby prams and plastic bags with food.

These macaques are not part of our zoo. If you encounter them, please keep all food out of sight.

They may approach you in search of food. Please remain calm and walk away.

Mandai Rainforest Resort
by Banyan Tree
Integrated Nature and Wildlife Destination

The Mandai Rainforest Resort by Banyan Tree is nestled within the Mandai Wildlife Reserve, Singapore’s nature and wildlife destination that is home to renowned zoological parks—Bird Paradise, Night Safari, River Wonders, Singapore Zoo, and Rainforest Wild ASIA—connecting people to the fascinating world of wildlife through meaningful experiences.

Biophilic design is at the heart of the resort’s architecture, influencing every aspect from programmes to signature amenities and services. Drawing from Banyan Group’s legacy of honouring the essence of each location, the 338-room resort blends seamlessly with its natural surroundings, offering rooms nestled in lush greenery or overlooking the Upper Seletar Reservoir. The unique layers of the rainforest—from the forest floor to the canopy—are reflected in the room designs, celebrating nature’s rich biodiversity.

Guests are encouraged to join in thoughtful sustainability efforts, fostering a lighter environmental footprint throughout their stay.

Visit Advisory

Due to large scale and private events happening on 6, 7, 20 & 21 Jun 2026, we anticipate limited parking spaces and high attendance at Rainforest Wild Adventure, Exploria and Curiosity Cove. You may experience foot traffic and potential changes to access routes or attraction timings. For the latest information, please refer to our Visit Advisory or check the Mandai App. 

6, 7 & 20 Jun 2026: Access to both Rainforest Wild Adventure EAST & WEST zones will not be available on these dates. 
7 & 21 Jun 2026: Operating hours of Exploria will be adjusted to 11am – 6pm (Last admission: 5pm). 
20 Jun 2026: Operating hours of Curiosity Cove will be adjusted to 3pm – 6.30pm (Last admission: 5.30pm), first two playtime sessions (10am and 12.30pm) will not be available.

Please note that Presentation schedule for Predators on Wings and Wings of the World may subject to changes in the event of large-scale programmings in the park. To enhance the overall experience for members, we encourage you to visit on less crowded days to experience the aviaries in a more intimate and personalised manner.

You are required to book an admission slot for visit to the Curiosity Cove, Exploria and Night Safari via Membership Portal. Each 20-digit membership visual ID may book up to 3 admission slots at any one time, subject to availability. If you change your mind about your visit, please update your booking on the Membership Portal so that others in the member community may visit instead.

To enjoy presentations at River Wonders and Night Safari, please reserve your seats online.

Check out the Do’s and Don’ts before embarking on your journey into Rainforest Wild Adventure.
